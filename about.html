How we got here

Williams Electrical started with a simple idea: that local trades work best when there's one person you actually deal with. Not a call centre, not a quote-by-text bot, not a different sub-contractor each time. Just a qualified electrician who answers the phone, comes when he says he will, and stands behind the work.

Alfie's been in the trade for over 7 years — first as an apprentice, then through his NVQs and AM2 assessment, then as a fully qualified domestic and commercial electrician under his own NICEIC registration. He works on jobs from a single socket to a full house rewire, and every one is treated the same way: properly planned, properly fitted, properly tested, properly certified.

What we believe

Local matters

We live and work in Warwick. We know the local housing stock — the 1960s semis with the original Wylex boards, the Victorian terraces with surface wiring under decades of paint, the new-builds where adding a socket means cutting through plasterboard you'd really rather not damage. Being local isn't a marketing line; it means you get someone who's done your kind of job a hundred times before.

Honest pricing, fixed quotes

Every job over the basic call-out is quoted in writing before work starts. Materials at trade plus a fair margin — no inflated mark-ups. If we find concealed problems mid-job (rotten cabling behind plaster, a broken back-box you couldn't see), we'll stop and tell you the cost before continuing. The price you agree is the price you pay.

Tidy site, no surprises

Sheet up before chasing. Hoover up before leaving. Floorboards screwed back down. Cables clipped, not draped. Plastered chases flush, not bulging. If you wouldn't want it in your own house, we wouldn't leave it in yours.
