Does your home actually need a rewire?

Not every old house needs a full rewire — that's an important thing to say upfront. We've talked plenty of homeowners out of unnecessary work after finding their existing wiring tests fine. But there are some clear indicators that a full or partial rewire is genuinely needed:

  • Rubber-insulated cabling (pre-1965) — the insulation degrades and cracks with age. If yours is this old, it's a non-negotiable replacement.
  • VIR or lead-sheathed cable — anything pre-1950. Often hidden in older Warwickshire properties.
  • Cloth/braided flex on lighting circuits — usually 1950s–60s and brittle.
  • Round-pin sockets (BS 546) — pre-1965 standard, now obsolete.
  • Twin-and-earth where the earth wire isn't sleeved green/yellow — pre-1977 cable, missing modern earthing.
  • Brown stains around sockets or switches — heat damage indicating overload or poor connections.
  • Failed EICR with major C1 or C2 codes across multiple circuits.

Full rewire vs. partial rewire

A full rewire replaces every cable, accessory, and the consumer unit — bringing the property fully up to BS 7671. Typical timescale: 5–10 working days for a 2–3 bedroom semi, longer for larger or more complex properties.

A partial rewire targets specific circuits — often just the lighting circuits, or just the kitchen/bathroom for a renovation. Significantly less disruptive and cheaper, suitable when the rest of the installation tests sound.

We'll never recommend a full rewire if a partial does the job. The Electrical Installation Condition Report (EICR) we carry out first is the honest baseline — it tells you exactly what needs replacing, what's borderline, and what's fine to leave.

What's included as standard

  • New consumer unit (dual-RCD or full RCBO).
  • All circuits rewired in modern twin-and-earth cable, properly clipped/run and zoned to BS 7671.
  • New accessories: sockets, switches, downlights — we offer a choice of finishes (white moulded, brushed steel, brushed brass).
  • Smoke and CO alarm system, mains-powered with battery backup, interlinked.
  • Bathroom IP-rated lighting and shaver sockets to current zonal regulations.
  • External sockets and lighting if required (kitchen extension, garden office, EV charger pre-wire).
  • Full electrical test on completion, with Electrical Installation Certificate.
  • Part P notification — Building Regulations sign-off in the post within 28 days.

Living through a rewire — minimising the disruption

We work room-by-room where possible so you can stay in the house. Every wall chase is repaired (we make-good in plaster — not just polyfilla over the top), every floorboard is screwed back down, and dust sheeting is used throughout. Where we can route cables through lofts or crawl spaces instead of chasing walls, we will — saves you redecorating costs.

For larger jobs we'll set out a daily plan: which rooms are off-supply, which circuits are live, when the power is back on for evenings. No surprises.
