What is an EICR?

An Electrical Installation Condition Report is a formal inspection of a property's fixed wiring — consumer unit, circuits, accessories, earthing, bonding — checked against the current BS 7671 standard. The output is a detailed written report grading every observation as:

  • C1 — Danger present. Risk of injury. Immediate action required.
  • C2 — Potentially dangerous. Urgent remedial action required.
  • C3 — Improvement recommended (but not required to pass).
  • FI — Further investigation needed.

A report with no C1, C2, or FI codes is "Satisfactory". Anything else is "Unsatisfactory" and remedial work is needed.

For landlords — the legal requirement

Since 1 April 2021, all private landlords in England must have an EICR carried out at least every 5 years, before a new tenancy starts, and provide the report to:

  • The tenant (within 28 days of inspection).
  • Any new tenant (before they move in).
  • The local authority on request (within 7 days).

Failure to comply can result in fines of up to £30,000. Any C1, C2, or FI observations must be remedied within 28 days (or sooner if specified in the report).

We carry out landlord EICRs across Warwickshire — same-day report, fixed-price quotes for any remedial work, and we work directly with letting agents if you'd rather we coordinate.

For property sales

An EICR isn't legally required to sell a house, but increasingly buyers' solicitors ask for one — particularly on older properties or when the buyer is taking out a mortgage. Having a current report makes the sale smoother and gives the buyer confidence. Costs are typically £150–£250 for a domestic property — small money for the speed it can add to a sale.

For homeowners — when to get one

  • Buying a home — strongly recommended pre-purchase, particularly anything pre-1990.
  • Every 10 years for owner-occupied homes (the IET recommendation).
  • After major renovation — adding extensions, kitchens, bathrooms.
  • If you've inherited a property or it's been unoccupied.
  • Any time you suspect problems — tripping circuits, scorching, etc.

What the inspection involves

A standard 3-bed domestic EICR takes 2–4 hours:

  • Visual inspection of consumer unit, accessories, fittings.
  • Sampling of cable joints, terminations, and earthing.
  • Insulation resistance test on every circuit.
  • Earth continuity, earth loop impedance, RCD trip-time testing.
  • Polarity checks, ring continuity on ring final circuits.
  • Written report with photographs, observations, and recommendations.

Some isolation is needed during testing — typically 5–10 minutes per circuit. We work around freezers, work-from-home, and anything else sensitive.
