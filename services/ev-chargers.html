Choosing the right charger for your home

A home EV charger isn't a one-size-fits-all decision. The right unit depends on three things: your supply capacity (single-phase or three-phase), the car you drive, and how you actually use it (mostly overnight on a tariff, or top-ups during the day).

We install all the major brands — Ohme, Hypervolt, Wallbox, Andersen, Easee, MyEnergi (Zappi), Project EV, Pod Point and others. Some specifics:

  • 7.4 kW (32A) single-phase — the standard. Fits about 95% of UK homes. Charges a typical EV from empty to full overnight (8–10 hours).
  • 22 kW (32A) three-phase — only worth it if you have three-phase supply (uncommon in UK domestic) and a car that can accept it. Most cars max out at 11 kW even with a three-phase charger.
  • Solar-integrated units (Zappi, Hypervolt 3.0) — divert excess solar generation to the car. Worth it if you already have solar PV.
  • Tariff-aware smart chargers (Ohme, Indra, Hypervolt) — schedule charging to off-peak windows automatically. Pays for the charger inside a year on Octopus Intelligent Go or Agile.

What we look at on a site survey

Every install starts with a 15–20 minute survey, either in person or via photos you send us. We confirm:

  • Your main fuse rating (60A, 80A, 100A) — informs load-balancing and whether we need to fit a CT clamp.
  • Your consumer unit — is there a spare way? Is it modern enough to take a 32A circuit, or do we need a sub-board?
  • The cable run from the consumer unit to the charger position. Shorter and tidier is cheaper.
  • The earthing arrangement (TN-C-S, TN-S, TT) — affects whether we can use PME for the charger or need an earth rod.
  • The charging point location — garage wall, external wall, drive post mount.

We send a fixed quote within 24 hours of the survey. No call-out fee.

The OZEV grant — who's eligible?

The original Electric Vehicle Homecharge Scheme (EVHS) closed to homeowners in April 2022, but the OZEV EV Chargepoint Grant still covers:

  • People who live in rented properties (with landlord permission) — up to £350 off.
  • People who live in flats (own or rent) — up to £350 off.

If you're an owner-occupier of a house, the grant unfortunately doesn't apply to you anymore. We'll always check eligibility and apply for the grant on your behalf where it does.
