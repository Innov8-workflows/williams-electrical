Common faults we deal with weekly

Electrical faults rarely happen at convenient times. Whether the kettle's just tripped the whole house, the bathroom lights have started flickering, or you've come home to find the freezer's off, we're set up to respond fast and diagnose properly — not just reset the trip and hope.

RCD won't reset

If your RCD keeps tripping the moment you reset it, there's a live fault somewhere on the circuit it protects. The trick is finding which circuit, which device, and what kind of fault — earth leakage, neutral-to-earth fault, dead short, or a faulty RCD itself. We carry insulation testers, clamp meters, and earth-loop testers — proper kit, not guesswork.

Half the house has no power

Usually one of two things: a tripped RCD or MCB, or a loose neutral connection at the consumer unit or a junction. The second is the dangerous one — a loose neutral causes voltage swings that damage appliances and, in the worst case, fires. We always check both.

Flickering or dimming lights

If lights dim when the kettle goes on, you've got a voltage drop somewhere — undersized cable, loose connection, or an overloaded circuit. If lights flicker randomly, it can be anything from a failing LED driver to a serious mains supply issue. We start with the easy stuff and escalate.

Burning smell from a socket or switch

Stop using the circuit. Call us straight away. A burning smell almost always means heat damage from a loose connection or overload — a precursor to an electrical fire. We'll be with you the same day where possible.

Buzzing sockets, hot dimmers, sparking when plugging in

Sometimes nuisance, sometimes serious. Buzzing is usually a loose terminal; hot dimmers are usually undersized for the load; a small spark when plugging in is normal, a sustained arc is not. We diagnose and fix in one visit where parts are available.
